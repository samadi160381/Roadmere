The Cooler That Keeps Up

Nothing kills a road trip faster than warm drinks and soggy sandwiches. Here's what actually keeps things cold, mile after mile.

Whether you're doing a weekend loop or a two-week cross-country haul, your cooler is doing more work than almost anything else in the car. We looked at ice retention, size-to-weight ratio, and real-world durability to narrow this down to five coolers worth the trunk space.

Best Overall

YETI Tundra 45

The Tundra 45 is the cooler most road trippers eventually land on. Rotomolded construction keeps ice solid for 4-5 days even in hot weather, and the latch system holds up to years of trunk abuse.

  • Holds ice for 4-5 days in summer heat
  • Fits 26 cans plus ice, or a mix of food and drinks
  • Bear-resistant certified for backcountry stops
Best Budget Pick

Coleman Xtreme 70 Quart

Not as rugged as a rotomolded cooler, but the Xtreme line punches well above its price point for straightforward weekend trips and family road trips where you're not gone more than 3-4 days.

  • Keeps ice for up to 5 days per Coleman's rating
  • Large enough for a family of four's food for a weekend
  • Wheeled version available for easier loading
Best for Long Trips

Dometic CFX3 55 (Electric)

This is a true portable fridge/freezer, not a passive cooler — it plugs into your car's 12V outlet or a portable power station and holds a set temperature indefinitely. Ideal for van lifers and anyone doing more than a week on the road.

  • No ice needed — runs off 12V or AC power
  • Adjustable from freezer to fridge temperatures
  • App-connected temperature monitoring
Best Compact Option

RTIC 20 Quart Hard Cooler

For couples or solo travelers who don't need a full-size cooler taking up trunk space, the RTIC 20 hits a sweet spot of ice retention and portability.

  • Compact enough for the back seat or a smaller trunk
  • Retains ice for 3-4 days
  • Roughly half the price of comparable YETI models

A Few Packing Tips

Pre-chill your cooler the night before with a bag of ice, then dump it out and pack for real in the morning — this alone can add a full day of ice retention. Pack drinks and things you'll grab often near the top, and use block ice rather than cubed when you can; it melts far slower.
