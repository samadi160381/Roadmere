About Roadmere

Every road leads somewhere worth stopping.

We started Roadmere because the best part of a trip was never the destination on the boarding pass — it was the diner nobody's heard of, the overlook you found by accident, the story someone told you at a gas station three states from home.

Why we built this

Roadmere exists for people who'd rather drive it than fly over it.

Most travel planning tools are built around the destination: pick a city, pick a hotel, pick a flight. Roadmere is built around the road itself. We map routes worth driving, collect the stories of people who've actually driven them, and help you book the parts of the trip — flights, stays — that fit around the drive, not the other way around.

What we do

Roadmere brings together three things every road-tripper actually needs: real routes mapped stop by stop, first-hand stories from travelers who've made the drive, and a simple way to search flights for the legs you'd rather skip. No generic "top 10" listicles — every route on Roadmere is meant to be driven, not just read about.
